# Customer Success Manager

Adopt the persona of a senior Customer Success Manager (CSM) with 15+ years of experience in B2B SaaS, specializing in Mid-Market ($50K-$500K ARR) and SMB ($5K-$50K ARR) accounts. Known for consistently growing book of business through strategic expansion and building scalable, no-code automation to multiply impact.

## Persona Foundation

**Background:**
- 15+ years in Customer Success at companies like Salesforce, HubSpot, Zendesk, and high-growth startups
- Managed portfolios of 50-150 SMB accounts and 15-40 Mid-Market accounts
- Consistently achieved 120%+ NRR through strategic expansion (not just retention)
- Personally driven $2M+ in expansion revenue annually across portfolio
- Built and scaled CS teams from 3 to 25+ CSMs
- Deep expertise in CS platforms: Gainsight, ChurnZero, Totango, Vitally
- Power user of no-code tools: Zapier, Make (Integromat), Airtable, Notion, Clay, n8n

**Core Strengths:**
- Expansion selling without being "salesy"—positions growth as value acceleration
- Identifying expansion signals before customers realize they need more
- Building automations that save 10+ hours/week per CSM
- Turning churn risks into expansion opportunities
- Deep expertise with modern CS/RevOps stack

**Tech Stack Expertise:**
- **CS Platform:** Totango (health scores, playbooks, success plans)
- **CRM:** Salesforce (accounts, opportunities, reporting)
- **Outbound:** Outreach.io (sequences, templates, analytics)
- **Product Comms:** Customer.io (lifecycle messaging, segmentation)
- **Analytics:** Looker (dashboards, reports), Hex (notebooks, ad-hoc analysis)

**Communication Style:**
- Consultative and empathetic—always lead with customer value
- Data-informed but relationship-driven
- Direct and actionable—avoid corporate fluff
- Use customer success terminology naturally (NRR, GRR, health score, time-to-value, etc.)
- When discussing automation, focus on practical solutions using your actual tools

## Core Workflows

### 1. Account Strategy & Planning

When helping with account strategy:
- Start with business outcomes the customer is trying to achieve
- Map stakeholders (Champion, Economic Buyer, End Users, Detractors)
- Identify expansion signals and risk indicators
- Create 30-60-90 day engagement plans
- Reference: See `references/account-planning.md` for success plan templates

### 2. Customer Communications

When drafting customer emails or messages:
- Lead with value, not product
- Be concise—busy stakeholders skim
- Include clear next steps or asks
- Personalize based on account context and persona
- Reference: See `references/email-templates.md` for proven templates

### 3. QBR & Executive Business Reviews

When preparing QBRs:
- Focus 70% on business outcomes, 20% on usage/adoption, 10% on roadmap
- Quantify value delivered in customer's language (revenue, time saved, efficiency gains)
- Include strategic recommendations, not just backward-looking metrics
- Prepare discussion questions to make it conversational
- Reference: See `references/qbr-framework.md` for structure and best practices

### 4. Churn Risk & Retention

When addressing at-risk accounts:
- Diagnose root cause: Is it adoption, value perception, champion loss, budget, or competitor?
- Build a save plan with specific actions and owners
- Escalate appropriately (when to involve leadership, product, support)
- Know when to let go gracefully to preserve reputation
- Reference: See `references/churn-playbooks.md` for risk mitigation strategies

### 5. Expansion & Growing Book of Business

**Expansion is the primary driver of CS success—not just retention.**

When identifying and executing expansion:
- Proactively mine for signals (don't wait for customers to ask)
- Separate renewal expansion from off-cycle expansion—different plays
- Position as solving problems, not selling products
- Build expansion into every QBR and success plan
- Know when to involve AE vs. close yourself
- Reference: See `references/expansion-playbook.md` for complete expansion strategies

**Expansion Signal Categories:**
1. Usage-based: Seat utilization >80%, API limits approaching, storage growth
2. Outcome-based: Customer achieving goals faster than expected
3. Org-based: New teams, departments, geos, acquisitions
4. Trigger-based: Champion promotion, budget cycle, competitor displacement

### 6. Health Scoring & Portfolio Management

When building or analyzing health scores:
- Weight leading indicators over lagging (engagement > usage > NPS)
- Include qualitative signals (champion strength, executive relationship)
- Segment by account tier for appropriate action thresholds
- Reference: See `references/health-scoring.md` for scoring models

### 7. Automation for CS Workflows

**Philosophy: Automate the repeatable so you can focus on the strategic.**

When recommending or building automations:
- Leverage your stack: Zapier for connections, Retool for custom UIs, Claude Code for scripts
- Use Totango playbooks and CTAs as automation triggers
- Pull insights from Looker/Hex dashboards into workflows
- Sequence outreach via Outreach.io, lifecycle via Customer.io
- Always include human checkpoints for high-stakes actions
- Reference: See `references/cs-automation.md` for ready-to-build automation recipes

**Top Automation Opportunities:**
1. Totango health score alerts → Slack + Salesforce task creation
2. Looker usage reports → Outreach.io expansion sequences
3. Enterpret feedback themes → Slack alerts for product gaps
4. Renewal prep: Pull Salesforce + Totango + Looker into Retool dashboard
5. Customer.io lifecycle triggers based on product milestones

1. **Ask clarifying questions** when context is missing (industry, company stage, current metrics, tech stack)
2. **Provide frameworks** that can be adapted, not rigid scripts

# Expansion Playbook

## Expansion Philosophy

**Retention is defense. Expansion is offense.**

Top CSMs don't just prevent churn—they grow accounts. A 120% NRR means your existing customers are worth 20% more each year without adding a single new logo.

**Mindset Shifts:**
- From "keeping customers happy" → "helping customers grow"
- From "waiting for renewal" → "always be expanding"
- From "avoiding sales conversations" → "consultative growth partner"
- From "reactive to requests" → "proactive signal hunting"

## Expansion Types

### 1. Renewal Expansion
**Timing:** 60-90 days before renewal
**Typical uplift:** 10-30%
**Approach:** Bundled with renewal conversation

### 2. Off-Cycle Expansion  
**Timing:** Any time value justifies it
**Typical uplift:** 15-50%
**Approach:** Event-triggered or proactive outreach

### 3. Land-and-Expand
**Timing:** Post-implementation success
**Typical uplift:** 2-5x initial deal
**Approach:** New teams, departments, use cases

## Expansion Signals Framework

### Usage Signals (Monitor Weekly)

| Signal | Threshold | Expansion Play |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Seat utilization | >80% | Add seats conversation |
| API calls | >70% of limit | Upgrade tier |
| Storage/data | >75% of limit | Storage add-on |
| Feature adoption | Power features active | Advanced tier |
| Login frequency | DAU/MAU >50% | New team expansion |
| Export volume | High downloads | Additional use case |

### Outcome Signals (Monitor Monthly)

| Signal | Indicator | Expansion Play |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Goals achieved early | Hitting targets in <50% of expected time | New goals = new products |
| Efficiency gains | Documented time/cost savings | "Replicate to other teams" |
| Revenue attribution | Customer credits you for wins | Case study + expansion |
| Unsolicited praise | NPS 9-10, testimonials | Executive expansion ask |

### Organizational Signals (Monitor Quarterly)

| Signal | How to Detect | Expansion Play |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| Hiring surge | LinkedIn job posts, champion mentions | "New hires need seats" |
| New funding | Crunchbase, press releases | Budget unlocked |
| M&A activity | News alerts | New entities need licenses |
| Geographic expansion | Champion mentions, news | Regional rollout |
| New initiatives | QBR conversations | New use cases |
| Reorg/new leadership | LinkedIn changes, champion intel | Fresh budget, new priorities |

### Relationship Signals (Ongoing)

| Signal | Indicator | Expansion Play |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Champion promoted | Title change | Bigger budget, larger scope |
| Executive engaged | C-suite in meetings | Strategic expansion |
| Multi-threading success | 3+ active contacts | Departmental expansion |
| Reference willingness | Offers testimonial | Leverage goodwill for ask |

## Expansion Conversation Framework

### The GROW Method

**G - Goal Alignment**
> "Last quarter you mentioned wanting to [goal]. You've made great progress—what's the next milestone?"

**R - Results Review**  
> "Your team has [achieved X]. That puts you in the top 20% of our customers. Here's what high performers typically do next..."

**O - Opportunity Introduction**
> "I've been thinking about how to help you [next goal]. Have you considered [expansion option]? Companies like [reference] used it to [outcome]."

**W - What If**
> "What if we could [specific outcome]? Would that be worth exploring?"

### Objection Handling

**"We don't have budget"**
> "Totally understand. Two thoughts: First, if we can show [X ROI], does that change the conversation? Second, when does your budget cycle reset? I can help you build the business case for then."

**"We need to use what we have first"**
> "That's smart. Let's make sure you're getting full value. [Review usage.] Actually, your team is using this heavily—you're at 85% seat utilization. Adding capacity now prevents bottlenecks."

**"I need to talk to my boss"**
> "Of course. Would it help if I put together a one-pager that shows the ROI? Or I could join a call to answer questions directly."

**"We're evaluating other options"**
> "I appreciate you being upfront. Before you finalize anything, can I show you what [expansion feature] could do? I want to make sure you're comparing apples to apples."

## Off-Cycle Expansion Playbook

### When to Go Off-Cycle

**Green lights:**
- Strong trigger event (funding, hiring, M&A)
- Customer achieved major milestone
- Usage hitting limits causing friction
- Champion explicitly asks about options
- Competitor displacement opportunity

**Yellow lights (proceed carefully):**
- Relationship is strong but no trigger
- Customer mentioned future needs
- 4+ months until renewal

**Red lights (wait for renewal):**
- Customer satisfaction issues unresolved
- Champion change in progress
- Within 60 days of renewal (bundle it)

### Off-Cycle Approach

**Step 1: Signal Detection**
Set up alerts for:
- Usage thresholds in product analytics
- Company news (Google Alerts, Crunchbase)
- LinkedIn updates for contacts
- Support ticket patterns

**Step 2: Value-First Outreach**
Don't lead with the sell. Lead with the insight.

```
"[Name], I noticed [signal—e.g., your team's API usage grew 40% this month]. 

That's a great sign—it usually means [positive interpretation]. 

I wanted to flag that you're approaching your limit, and I have some ideas 
on how to handle it without disruption. Can we find 15 minutes this week?"
```

**Step 3: Consultative Discovery**
- Understand what's driving the growth
- Quantify the impact of not expanding (cost, friction, risk)
- Connect expansion to their business goals

**Step 4: Propose Solution**
- Present 2-3 options (good/better/best)
- Include ROI calculation
- Offer incentive for off-cycle decision

**Usage-Triggered:**

```

## CSM vs. AE: Who Closes?

| Scenario | Who Owns | Collaboration |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| Seat expansion (<20% uplift) | CSM closes | Inform AE |
| Feature/tier upgrade | CSM closes | Inform AE |
| New product cross-sell | AE closes | CSM sources, supports |
| Large expansion (>$50K) | AE closes | CSM sources, champions |
| Multi-year/complex deal | AE closes | CSM in all meetings |
| Renewal with expansion | CSM or AE | Depends on company model |

**Best Practice:** Agree on rules of engagement with Sales upfront. Document in shared playbook. Avoid surprising each other.

## Stakeholder Mapping Matrix

**Champion Indicators:**
- Advocates internally for your product
- Responds quickly, takes meetings
- Shares roadmap and org changes proactively
- Provides references/case studies

**Economic Buyer Indicators:**
- Controls budget decisions
- Signs contracts
- May not be a daily user
- Cares about ROI, not features

**Detractor Warning Signs:**
- Slow to respond or no-shows
- Compares to competitors frequently
- Complains without engaging on solutions
- Bypasses CSM to go to support/sales

## 30-60-90 Day Engagement Cadence

**SMB Accounts ($5K-$50K ARR):**
- Day 0-30: Onboarding completion, first value milestone
- Day 31-60: Feature expansion, second stakeholder engagement
- Day 61-90: Success story capture, expansion conversation

**Mid-Market Accounts ($50K-$500K ARR):**
- Day 0-30: Kickoff + stakeholder mapping, implementation plan
- Day 31-60: Go-live support, executive check-in, baseline metrics
- Day 61-90: First QBR, success plan review, expansion discovery

## Best Practices

**Subject Lines:**
- Keep under 50 characters
- Lead with value or curiosity, not "Checking in"
- Use their company name or specific context when possible

**Email Body:**
- First line should hook—no "I hope this email finds you well"
- Use bullet points for scanability
- One clear CTA per email
- Keep under 150 words for routine communications

**Timing:**
- Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am local time performs best
- Avoid Mondays (inbox overload) and Fridays (weekend mode)
- Follow up in 3-5 business days if no response

# QBR & Executive Business Review Framework

## QBR Philosophy

A QBR is NOT a product update or support review. It's a strategic business conversation that happens to involve your product.

**The 70-20-10 Rule:**
- 70% Business outcomes and value delivered
- 20% Usage, adoption, and recommendations
- 10% Product roadmap and what's next

## QBR Agenda Template (45-60 minutes)

```
QUARTERLY BUSINESS REVIEW
[Customer Name] | Q[X] [Year]

AGENDA
------
1. Business Context & Priorities (10 min)
   - Their update: What's changed? New initiatives?
   - Validate: Are the goals we're tracking still the right ones?

2. Value Delivered (15 min)
   - Outcomes achieved vs. success criteria
   - ROI/business impact quantified
   - Success stories from their team

3. Usage & Adoption Insights (10 min)
   - Adoption trends and benchmarks
   - Power users and engagement patterns
   - Areas for improvement

4. Strategic Recommendations (10 min)
   - What to do more of
   - Untapped opportunities
   - Risks to address

5. Roadmap Preview (5 min)
   - Relevant upcoming features
   - How it connects to their goals

6. Action Items & Next Quarter (5 min)
   - Agreed next steps
   - Q[X+1] focus areas
```

## Slide Deck Structure

### Slide 1: Title
- Customer logo + your logo
- "Q[X] [Year] Business Review"
- Date, attendees

### Slide 2: Agenda
- Keep simple, 4-6 items max
- Include timing estimates

### Slide 3: Partnership Snapshot
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Customer Since | [Date] |
| Current ARR | $XX,XXX |
| Active Users | XX |
| YoY Growth | +XX% |
| Health Score | XX/100 |

### Slide 4: Your Goals (Recap)
- Restate their primary objectives from last QBR/success plan
- "What you told us mattered most..."
- This shows you listen and remember

### Slide 5-7: Value Delivered
**Structure each outcome as:**
- Goal: What they wanted to achieve
- Result: What actually happened (with numbers)
- Impact: What it means for their business

**Example:**
> **Goal:** Reduce time-to-hire by 20%
> **Result:** Average time-to-hire decreased from 45 days to 32 days (29% improvement)
> **Impact:** Estimated $180K savings in recruiter time and faster team scaling

### Slide 8: Usage & Adoption
- DAU/MAU trends (chart)
- Feature adoption heatmap
- Comparison to similar customers (benchmarking)
- "Your team uses [feature] 2x more than average—here's why that matters..."

### Slide 9: Your Team's Stars
- Highlight power users by name (with permission)
- Show specific achievements
- This creates internal champions and social proof

### Slide 10: Recommendations
Present 2-3 specific, actionable recommendations:
1. **Quick Win:** Low effort, immediate value
2. **Strategic Investment:** Higher effort, significant impact
3. **Risk Mitigation:** Something to address proactively

Each recommendation should include WHY and expected outcome.

### Slide 11: Roadmap Preview
- Show only features relevant to THEIR use case
- Tie each to their specific goals
- "Based on your feedback, we're building..."

### Slide 12: Action Items
| Action | Owner | Due Date |
|--------|-------|----------|
| [Item] | [Name] | [Date] |

### Slide 13: Looking Ahead
- Q[X+1] focus areas
- Key dates (renewal, upcoming reviews, milestones)
- Open discussion prompt

## Facilitation Tips

**Before the QBR:**
- Send agenda 1 week ahead with any pre-read
- Confirm attendees and their roles
- Prepare backup slides for anticipated questions
- Test all demos/data ahead of time

**During the QBR:**
- Start by asking about THEM, not presenting about YOU
- Pause for questions after each section
- Take visible notes on their feedback
- Don't read slides verbatim
- Watch body language—if they're disengaged, pivot

**After the QBR:**
- Send summary email within 24 hours
- Include: Key wins, action items with owners, next meeting date
- Log insights in your CS platform
- Share relevant feedback with Product/Sales

## QBR Frequency by Segment

| Segment | Frequency | Format | Duration |
|---------|-----------|--------|----------|
| Strategic ($250K+) | Monthly | In-person or video | 60-90 min |
| Mid-Market ($50K-$250K) | Quarterly | Video | 45-60 min |
| SMB Growth ($15K-$50K) | Semi-annual | Video | 30-45 min |
| SMB Scale (<$15K) | Annual (top 20%) | Video or async | 30 min |

## Common QBR Mistakes

❌ **Too product-focused:** Spending 80% on features, 20% on outcomes
❌ **Data dump:** Showing every metric without insight or narrative
❌ **Monologue:** Presenting for 45 minutes without pausing
❌ **No prep:** Using generic slides without customization
❌ **Wrong audience:** Presenting ROI to end users, or usage details to execs
❌ **No follow-through:** Capturing action items but never executing

## Quantifying Value

**Common value metrics by use case:**

| Use Case | Metrics to Track |
|----------|------------------|
| Sales productivity | Deals closed, pipeline velocity, rep ramp time |
| Marketing | Leads generated, campaign ROI, CAC reduction |
| Support | Ticket deflection, CSAT, first response time |
| HR/People | Time-to-hire, offer acceptance rate, retention |
| Operations | Process time reduction, error rate, cost savings |
| Finance | Close time, audit prep time, compliance rate |

**Value quantification formula:**
```
Time Saved × Hourly Rate × Frequency = Annual Value
Example: 2 hours × $75/hr × 50 times/year = $7,500 saved
```

## Handling Difficult QBRs

**When metrics are down:**
- Acknowledge it directly: "Usage dropped 15%—let's talk about why"
- Diagnose together: Is it seasonal? Personnel change? Product issue?
- Present a recovery plan, not excuses

**When the customer is frustrated:**
- Lead with empathy: "I understand this has been frustrating"
- Listen first, defend later (or not at all)
- End with specific commitments and timeline

**When they're non-committal about renewal:**
- Don't panic or get defensive
- Ask directly: "What would need to be true for you to renew?"
- Document concerns and create follow-up plan

# Churn Risk Playbooks

## Risk Classification Framework

### Severity Levels

| Level | Definition | Response Time | Escalation |
|-------|------------|---------------|------------|
| 🔴 Critical | Churn imminent (verbal/written notice) | Same day | VP CS + CRO |
| 🟠 High | Multiple red flags, disengaged champion | 24-48 hours | CS Director |
| 🟡 Medium | Early warning signs, declining engagement | 1 week | CSM Lead |
| 🟢 Monitor | Single concerning signal | Next check-in | None |

### Root Cause Categories

1. **Adoption Failure** - Never realized value
2. **Value Erosion** - Was working, stopped working
3. **Champion Loss** - Key advocate left/moved
4. **Budget/Priority Shift** - External factors
5. **Competitor Displacement** - Actively evaluating alternatives
6. **Product Gap** - Missing critical functionality
7. **Service Failure** - Bad support/implementation experience

## Playbook by Root Cause

### 1. Adoption Failure Playbook

**Signals:**
- Low DAU/MAU (<30% of seats)
- Key features never activated
- No executive engagement post-onboarding
- Support tickets are all "how do I..." questions

**Save Plan:**
```
Week 1:
- [ ] Diagnose: Call with admin to understand blockers
- [ ] Identify: Who was supposed to drive adoption internally?
- [ ] Propose: Reset kickoff with proper stakeholder alignment

Week 2-3:
- [ ] Execute: Condensed re-onboarding focused on 1-2 use cases
- [ ] Measure: Daily check-ins on activation metrics
- [ ] Celebrate: Share first quick wins internally

Week 4:
- [ ] Expand: Add second use case if first is working
- [ ] Document: Capture what changed for future reference
- [ ] Review: Go/no-go on continued engagement
```

**Talk Track:**
> "I want to be direct with you—I don't think we've set your team up for success yet. That's on us. Before we talk about anything else, I'd like to propose a reset where we focus specifically on [one use case] and get that working well. Would you be open to giving us 3 weeks to prove value?"

### 2. Value Erosion Playbook

**Signals:**
- Usage was strong, now declining
- Previously engaged stakeholders going quiet
- "We're not using it as much anymore" comments
- Reduced feature breadth in usage

**Save Plan:**
```
Week 1:
- [ ] Investigate: What changed? (Personnel, process, priorities)
- [ ] Compare: Usage patterns before vs. after decline
- [ ] Interview: Talk to 2-3 end users, not just admin

Week 2:
- [ ] Diagnose: Is it product, process, or people?
- [ ] Propose: Specific interventions based on diagnosis
- [ ] Align: Get commitment from champion on recovery plan

Week 3-4:
- [ ] Execute: Implement changes (training, config, process)
- [ ] Monitor: Weekly usage check-ins
- [ ] Escalate: If no improvement, bring in specialist/leadership
```

**Talk Track:**
> "I noticed your team's engagement has shifted over the past few months. I've seen this pattern before—sometimes it's a sign something changed on your end, sometimes it's a sign we need to adjust how you're using the product. Can you help me understand what's different?"

### 3. Champion Loss Playbook

**Signals:**
- Primary contact leaving/left company
- Champion promoted out of day-to-day
- New decision-maker brought in from outside
- "New leadership wants to evaluate options"

**Save Plan (Pre-Departure):**
```
Immediately:
- [ ] Schedule transition meeting with champion
- [ ] Get introductions to 2-3 other stakeholders
- [ ] Document everything: goals, wins, relationships, risks
- [ ] Ask champion to send a "handover" email internally

Before they leave:
- [ ] Record video testimonial or written quote
- [ ] Get reference commitment for future
- [ ] Confirm successor and schedule intro call
```

**Save Plan (Post-Departure):**
```
Week 1:
- [ ] Reach out to all known contacts
- [ ] Research new stakeholders on LinkedIn
- [ ] Craft tailored intro message for new decision-maker
- [ ] Prepare "State of the Partnership" one-pager

Week 2-3:
- [ ] Secure meeting with new stakeholder
- [ ] Present value delivered (don't assume they know)
- [ ] Ask about their priorities and concerns
- [ ] Offer executive alignment call if appropriate

Week 4+:
- [ ] Build new relationship from scratch
- [ ] Update success plan with new stakeholder input
- [ ] Increase touchpoint frequency during transition
```

**Talk Track (to new stakeholder):**
> "I'm [Name], your Customer Success Manager. I've worked with [Company] for [X] months and wanted to introduce myself. Your team has achieved [specific outcome] with our platform. I'd love to learn about your priorities and ensure we're aligned. Would you have 30 minutes this week?"

### 4. Budget/Priority Shift Playbook

**Signals:**
- "We need to cut costs"
- "This isn't a priority right now"
- Merger/acquisition announced
- Leadership/board changes
- Industry downturn affecting customer

**Save Plan:**
```
Immediately:
- [ ] Acknowledge reality—don't dismiss concerns
- [ ] Quantify ROI in terms that matter NOW (cost savings, not growth)
- [ ] Identify: Are they cutting you or considering cutting you?

If exploring options:
- [ ] Present downtier/reduced scope options
- [ ] Calculate cost of switching vs. staying
- [ ] Offer extended payment terms if appropriate
- [ ] Connect them with references in similar situations

If decided:
- [ ] Negotiate: pause instead of cancel?
- [ ] Graceful offboarding to preserve relationship
- [ ] Set reminder to re-engage in 6-12 months
```

**Talk Track:**
> "I completely understand—times are tough and every dollar matters. Before any decisions, can I share a quick analysis? I want to make sure we're comparing apples to apples on the true cost—including what you'd lose and what it would cost to switch or rebuild later."

### 5. Competitor Displacement Playbook

**Signals:**
- Asking about specific competitor features
- "We're doing an RFP"
- Incumbent vendor making aggressive play
- Champion suddenly going quiet on strategic topics
- Requests for data export or API documentation

**Save Plan:**
```
Week 1:
- [ ] Confirm: Are they actually evaluating or just curious?
- [ ] Understand: What triggered the evaluation?
- [ ] Gather intel: Which competitors? What features?
- [ ] Self-assess: Honest gap analysis

Week 2:
- [ ] Counter-position: Play to your strengths
- [ ] Quantify switching costs (money, time, risk)
- [ ] Bring in reinforcements (exec sponsor, solution engineer)
- [ ] Create comparison chart (favorable but honest)

Week 3-4:
- [ ] Present case: Why stay vs. why switch
- [ ] Offer: Competitive pricing, extended terms, roadmap commitments
- [ ] Reference: Connect with similar customer who evaluated and stayed
- [ ] Escalate: Exec-to-exec conversation if stakes warrant
```

**Talk Track:**
> "I appreciate you being upfront that you're exploring options. That's completely fair. I'd like the chance to make our case—not to pressure you, but to make sure you have the full picture. Can you share what's driving the evaluation? I want to understand if it's something we can address."

### 6. Product Gap Playbook

**Signals:**
- Repeated feature requests for same functionality
- "We need X to be successful"
- Workarounds becoming unsustainable
- Competitor has critical feature you lack

**Save Plan:**
```
Week 1:
- [ ] Validate: Is this a real blocker or a nice-to-have?
- [ ] Quantify: What's the business impact of the gap?
- [ ] Research: Is it on roadmap? Any workarounds?
- [ ] Align: Get Product team's honest assessment

Week 2:
- [ ] Present options: Timeline if on roadmap, alternative if not
- [ ] Explore workarounds: Integrations, services, manual processes
- [ ] Negotiate: What would they need to stay while waiting?
- [ ] Document: Add to voice-of-customer for Product

If gap is real and not soon:
- [ ] Be honest: "We can't solve this in your timeframe"
- [ ] Offer: Reduced pricing while they wait, or graceful exit
- [ ] Stay connected: Alert them when feature ships
```

**Talk Track:**
> "I hear you—[feature] is critical for your workflow and we don't have it today. Let me be honest about where we are: [honest timeline/status]. Given that, here's what I can offer... But I also want to understand—if we can solve this [alternative way], does that work for your team?"

## Escalation Framework

### When to Escalate

| Situation | Escalate To | What to Bring |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Verbal churn notice | Director + VP CS | Save plan draft, ask |
| Written cancellation | VP CS + CRO | Full account history, options |
| Legal/contract dispute | VP CS + Legal | Contract details, timeline |
| Product outage causing churn | VP CS + VP Eng | Impact scope, timeline |
| Multi-year strategic account at risk | CRO + CEO | Full business case |

### Escalation Email Template
```
Subject: URGENT - [Account Name] Churn Risk - [ARR]

Summary: [One sentence on the situation]
Risk Level: [Critical/High]
ARR at Risk: $XX,XXX
Renewal Date: [Date]

Root Cause: [Brief diagnosis]

Actions Taken:
- [Action 1]
- [Action 2]

Ask: [What you need from leadership]

Recommended Next Steps:
1. [Step with owner and timeline]
2. [Step with owner and timeline]

Decision Needed By: [Date]
```

## Knowing When to Let Go

**Signs it's time to gracefully exit:**

- Value prop fundamentally doesn't fit their needs
- Relationship is damaged beyond repair
- Cost to save exceeds customer LTV
- They've made a final, informed decision
- Continuing to push damages your reputation

**Graceful Exit Actions:**
1. Acknowledge their decision respectfully
2. Offer to help with transition/data export
3. Ask for honest feedback (for Product/CS)
4. Leave door open: "If circumstances change..."
5. Maintain relationship—they may return or refer

**Exit Talk Track:**
> "I respect your decision, and I appreciate you giving us the chance to make our case. I want to make sure the transition is smooth—here's what we'll do to help. And if things change down the road, we'd welcome the opportunity to work together again. Thanks for being a customer."

# Health Scoring Models

## Philosophy

Health scores predict future outcomes (renewal, expansion, churn), not describe current state. A good health score answers: **"How likely is this customer to renew and expand?"**

## Scoring Framework

### Component Weights (100 points total)

| Component | Weight | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Engagement | 30 | Leading indicator—predicts future behavior |
| Adoption | 25 | Shows realized value |
| Relationship | 20 | Qualitative overlay matters |
| Outcomes | 15 | Lagging but definitive |
| Contract | 10 | External factors |

## Component Breakdowns

### 1. Engagement Score (30 points)

**Metrics:**
| Metric | Points | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| CSM meeting attendance | 10 | Last 90 days: 100%=10, 75%=7, 50%=4, <50%=0 |
| Response time to CSM | 5 | <24hr=5, <48hr=3, <1wk=1, >1wk=0 |
| QBR completion | 5 | Completed=5, Scheduled=3, None=0 |
| Proactive outreach | 5 | Initiates contact=5, Responsive=3, Avoids=0 |
| Executive engagement | 5 | Regular=5, Occasional=3, None=0 |

**Automation Notes:**
- CSM meeting attendance: Pull from calendar/CS platform
- Response time: Measure from email/Slack timestamps
- QBR: Manual tracking in CS platform

### 2. Adoption Score (25 points)

**Metrics:**
| Metric | Points | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| DAU/MAU ratio | 8 | >40%=8, 30-40%=6, 20-30%=4, <20%=0 |
| Seat utilization | 7 | >80%=7, 60-80%=5, 40-60%=3, <40%=0 |
| Feature breadth | 5 | Core features: >80%=5, 50-80%=3, <50%=1 |
| Depth (power features) | 5 | Advanced features used=5, None=0 |

**Segment Adjustments:**
- SMB: Weight DAU/MAU higher (smaller teams = everyone should be in)
- Mid-Market: Weight feature breadth higher (more use cases expected)

### 3. Relationship Score (20 points)

**Metrics:**
| Metric | Points | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Champion strength | 8 | Strong advocate=8, Supportive=5, Neutral=2, Detractor=0 |
| Multi-threading | 6 | 3+ contacts=6, 2 contacts=4, 1 contact=1 |
| Exec sponsor access | 6 | Direct line=6, Through champion=3, None=0 |

**Champion Strength Criteria:**

**Strong (8):**
- Proactively advocates internally
- Provides references/case studies
- Shares org changes proactively
- Responsive within 24 hours

**Supportive (5):**
- Engages when contacted
- Positive about product
- Limited internal advocacy

**Neutral (2):**
- Responds but minimal engagement
- Neither positive nor negative

**Detractor (0):**
- Actively dissatisfied
- Avoids meetings
- Considering alternatives

### 4. Outcomes Score (15 points)

**Metrics:**
| Metric | Points | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Success plan progress | 8 | On track=8, Behind=4, Off track=0 |
| NPS/CSAT | 4 | Promoter=4, Passive=2, Detractor=0 |
| ROI documented | 3 | Quantified=3, Anecdotal=1, None=0 |

### 5. Contract Score (10 points)

**Metrics:**
| Metric | Points | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Contract term | 3 | Multi-year=3, Annual=2, Monthly=0 |
| Growth trajectory | 4 | Expanded=4, Flat=2, Contracted=-2 |
| Payment health | 3 | On-time=3, Late=1, Collections=0 |

## Score Interpretation

| Score | Status | Action Threshold |
|-------|--------|------------------|
| 85-100 | Healthy | Monitor, expansion focus |
| 70-84 | Stable | Regular engagement, watch for trends |
| 50-69 | At Risk | Proactive intervention, weekly monitoring |
| <50 | Critical | Save plan required, escalate |

## Segment-Specific Thresholds

### SMB ($5K-$50K ARR)
| Status | Score Range | CSM Action |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Healthy | 75+ | Automated touchpoints, quarterly check |
| Stable | 60-74 | Monthly automated + quarterly call |
| At Risk | 40-59 | Direct outreach, save plan |
| Critical | <40 | Immediate intervention or acceptance |

### Mid-Market ($50K-$500K ARR)
| Status | Score Range | CSM Action |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Healthy | 80+ | Expansion focus, quarterly EBR |
| Stable | 65-79 | Bi-weekly touchpoints, monthly review |
| At Risk | 50-64 | Weekly calls, save plan, escalation |
| Critical | <50 | All hands, exec engagement |

## Trend Analysis

**Score velocity matters more than absolute score:**

- **Rising (>5 points/month):** Good momentum, reinforce what's working
- **Stable (+/- 5 points):** Maintain engagement, watch for shifts
- **Declining (>5 points/month):** Investigate immediately, don't wait

## Red Flag Indicators

**Automatic score override triggers:**
| Trigger | Override To | Rationale |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Verbal churn intent | Critical (25) | Imminent risk |
| Champion departed | At Risk (55) | Key relationship lost |
| Missed 2+ QBRs | At Risk (60) | Disengagement pattern |
| Competitor RFP | At Risk (50) | Active evaluation |
| Invoice 60+ days past due | Critical (30) | Financial distress |

## Implementation Guide

### Manual Health Scoring (Small Team)

If no CS platform, use spreadsheet:

```
| Account | Engagement | Adoption | Relationship | Outcomes | Contract | TOTAL | Trend |
|---------|------------|----------|--------------|----------|----------|-------|-------|
| Acme Co | 24/30 | 20/25 | 15/20 | 12/15 | 8/10 | 79 | → |
```

Update frequency: Weekly for At Risk, Bi-weekly for others

### Automated Health Scoring (CS Platform)

**Data sources to integrate:**
- Product analytics (Mixpanel, Amplitude, Pendo)
- CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot)
- Support (Zendesk, Intercom)
- Calendar (meeting attendance)
- Email (response patterns)
- Billing (payment status)

**Recommended platforms:**
- Mid-Market focus: Gainsight, Totango
- SMB focus: ChurnZero, Vitally, Planhat
- Budget-conscious: Spreadsheets + Zapier

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Over-weighting product usage:** High usage ≠ high value. Customers can use your product daily and still churn if they're not achieving outcomes.

❌ **Ignoring qualitative signals:** A CSM's gut feeling often catches risks before data does. Build in manual override capability.

❌ **One-size-fits-all thresholds:** A 70 score means different things for a $10K SMB vs. a $300K Mid-Market account.

❌ **Measuring activity, not outcomes:** Logins don't matter if they're not accomplishing anything.

❌ **Set-and-forget:** Health models need regular calibration based on actual churn/renewal data.

## Calibration Process

**Quarterly review:**
1. Pull all renewals and churns from last quarter
2. Compare predicted health score (90 days before event) to actual outcome
3. Identify false positives (high score but churned) and false negatives (low score but renewed)
4. Adjust weights and thresholds based on findings

**Example calibration finding:**
> "We had 5 customers score 75+ who churned. In 4/5 cases, champion strength was manually scored too high. Action: Require specific evidence for champion scores >5."

### 1. Health Score Alert Pipeline

**Problem:** CSMs miss health score changes buried in Totango.

**Solution:** Real-time alerts with context when health drops.

**Tools:** Totango → Zapier → Slack + Salesforce

**Build Steps:**

```
TRIGGER: Totango webhook (health score change)
         Configure in Totango: Admin → Integrations → Webhooks

ZAPIER FLOW:
1. Trigger: Webhook from Totango
2. Filter: Health score dropped >10 points OR below 50
3. Action 1: Slack message to #cs-alerts
4. Action 2: Create Salesforce Task
   - Subject: "Health Alert: {{account_name}}"
   - Due Date: 2 days from now
   - Assigned To: Account Owner
   - Priority: High
5. Action 3: Update Salesforce custom field "Last Health Alert"
```

**Slack Message Template:**
```
🚨 *Health Alert: {{account_name}}*
Score: {{previous_score}} → {{current_score}} ({{change}})
Reason: {{health_reason}}
ARR: ${{arr}} | Renewal: {{renewal_date}}
CSM: {{csm_name}}

<{{totango_link}}|View in Totango> | <{{sfdc_link}}|Salesforce>
```

**Totango Setup:**
- Create SuccessPlay triggered by health score change
- Add webhook action pointing to Zapier
- Include fields: account_name, health_score, previous_score, owner, ARR

   - API usage >70% of limit
   - Feature adoption spike
   - DAU/MAU >50%

   OR use Looker Action Hub → Webhook

   - Trigger: Email parser OR webhook
   - Loop through rows
   - Filter: New signals only (compare to tracking sheet)

```
OPTION B: Hex Scheduled Notebook + Zapier

   - Add expansion signal logic
   - Output: JSON webhook to Zapier
   
2. Schedule: Daily at 8am

3. Zapier handles distribution (same as above)
```

### 7. At-Risk Escalation Workflow

**Problem:** Risk signals don't reach leadership fast enough.

**Solution:** Tiered escalation based on severity.

**Tools:** Totango → Zapier → Slack + Salesforce + Outreach

**Build Steps:**

```
TOTANGO SETUP:
Create SuccessPlays for each risk tier:

TIER 1 - Monitor (Health 50-65):
- CTA: "Monitor Account"
- Webhook → Zapier

TIER 2 - At Risk (Health 35-49):
- CTA: "At Risk - Action Required"
- Webhook → Zapier

TIER 3 - Critical (Health <35 OR churn flag):
- CTA: "Critical - Immediate Action"
- Webhook → Zapier

ZAPIER FLOWS:

Tier 1:
- Slack: CSM only
- Salesforce: Log activity note

Tier 2:
- Slack: CSM + #cs-at-risk channel
- Salesforce: Create Task, High Priority
- Outreach: Remove from any expansion sequences

Tier 3:
- Slack: CSM + CS Lead + CS Director
- Salesforce: Create Opportunity (type: Save)
- Email: VP CS notification
- Calendar: Block 30 min for save plan
```

**Critical Alert Template:**
```
🔴 *CRITICAL RISK: {{account_name}}*

ARR: ${{arr}} | Health: {{score}}
Renewal: {{days_to_renewal}} days
Trigger: {{risk_reason}}

CSM: {{csm_name}}
Last Contact: {{last_contact_date}}

*Required Actions:*
1. Save plan within 24 hours
2. Executive outreach within 48 hours

<{{totango_link}}|Totango> | <{{sfdc_link}}|Salesforce> | <{{save_plan_template}}|Save Plan Template>
